Falls under the district of Seoni and Chhindwara, Pench National Park is undoubtedly one of the star attractions of Madhya Pradesh. The park is well-known for its opulent fauna.

Most commonly found prey species in Pench National Park include Indian wild dog, jungle cat, wild boar, chital, jackal, hyena, and wolf. Deer, rhesus macaque, langur, gaur, sambar, and bison can also be seen in the herd in the forest. In total, there are more than 210 species of bird in Pench National Park.
